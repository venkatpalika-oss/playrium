# Experience Evaluation Template (Phase 1)

Every Playrium Original begins here. Copy this template for each new concept before any design work begins.

## Concept
One or two sentences describing what the experience is and what the user does.

## Why It Deserves to Exist
What gap in the current catalogue does this fill? Why should this be built now rather than later, or not at all?

## Similar Products or Precedents
What existing apps, toys, or experiences does this resemble or draw inspiration from? Naming precedents honestly helps clarify what would actually be original about the Playrium version.

## Differentiation
What makes the Playrium version distinct from its precedents? This should go beyond visual polish and identify a genuine interaction or emotional difference.

## Emotional Journey
Sketch the intended arc: arrival, curiosity, play, absorption, anticipation, Magic Moment, reflection, replay. Identify the intended Magic Moment specifically, even at this early stage.

## Risks
What could make this concept fail? Consider technical risk, novelty risk where nobody understands the interaction, and flatness risk where the build is technically fine but emotionally unremarkable.

## Technical Complexity
Rough estimate of build difficulty and any new techniques or performance concerns this concept would introduce.

## Long-Term Value
Does this concept only work as a one-off, or does it suggest a family of future variations, seasonal content, or shareable outputs that extend its life?

## Go / No-Go Decision
State the decision explicitly: proceed to Phase 2, Experience Design; hold for later; or reject. Include the one or two factors that most influenced the decision.
